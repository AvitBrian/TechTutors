About Us

Digital illiteracy remains a significant barrier to accessing information and staying updated in today's digital world, especially in Africa. Everything from cash to social events has been moved into the digital world to counter obstacles of different nature like the pandemic. Although many countries in Africa have taken the first step to enlightenment in this matter, there are still huge gaps that need to be closed. Alice Kagina (2022) In Rwanda,  only 15% of the population has smartphones. This means that approximately 1 in every 7 people has the privilege of owning a smartphone. This might not seem like a big deal but According to a report published by comScore, a leading analytics company, in February 2012, smartphone penetration in the United States surpassed 50% for the first time. In the United States, the milestone of over 50% of the population using smartphones was reached in early 2012. This means after 11 years, Rwanda has 15% of its population using smartphones. Many individuals, particularly those with limited resources or lacking digital skills, struggle to keep pace with the latest trends, valuable tips, and information that can improve their daily lives. While we can not give out smartphones to the remaining eleven million we can help by Bridging the gap and providing inclusive access to relevant information for empowering individuals and fostering digital literacy.
TechTutors is a web-based platform addressing digital illiteracy and providing inclusive access to relevant information in Africa. The project aims to bridge the gap by offering valuable tips, the latest trends, and diverse information through a mobile-friendly platform and USSD application. The target audience includes individuals with limited digital skills or resources. The intended outcome is to empower users by fostering digital literacy.

Our Goal

  • To provide a user-friendly platform for accessing valuable information and fostering African digital literacy.
  • Develop a mobile-friendly platform accessible through smartphones and USSD applications.
  • Create diverse information categories covering technology, arts, education, and more.
  • Deliver concise, easy-to-understand content in the form of short articles, infographics, and videos.
  • Foster user-generated content and encourage community participation.
  • Incorporate offline accessibility features for users with limited internet connectivity (Downloadable Resource )
